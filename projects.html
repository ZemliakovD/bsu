PROJECTS AND RESEARCH

Research work at the Department of Systems Analysis and Computer Simulation of the Faculty of Radiophysics and Computer Technologies is conducted in accordance with The following priority areas of basic and applied research:

Bioinformatics

Modeling of processes in complex physical and biomolecular systems

Analysis of fluorescence fluctuation spectroscopy data

Analysis of biomedical images

Student work is also mainly carried out on this topic.

The most significant achievements in research and innovation activities of the Department of System Analysis and Computer Modeling in 2018:

A statistical approach has been developed to predict alternative splicing events in primary mRNA of human genes. Using examples of model genes, the efficiency of the developed approach was confirmed and it was shown that the accuracy of RNA product separation is 99.5% for the considered pairs of model non-homologous human genes. It has been established that experimentally confirmed, as well as predicted transcripts do not form separate clearly separated clusters for human oncogene RUNX1 / RUNX1T1

In the field of basic scientific research:

A parallel software package RNAexploreR has been developed to predict alternative splicing variants in primary mRNAs of the human RUNX1 / RUNX1T1 chimeric oncogene;

The process of physical adsorption of a liquid on the surface of a mesoporous structure was investigated as a result of computer simulation in the physical reconstruction of Si12;

A diagnostic technique has been developed for biological compounds using optical sensor matrices of the resonator type.

Orfhunter

ORFHunteR - a web-service that finds Open Reading Frames (ORFs) in RNA sequences. Use ORFhunteR to search newly sequenced RNA for potential protein encoding segments.

Read more
Rnaexplorer

RNAexploreR is a computational tool for studding the alternative splicing of RNA molecules of normal and mutant human genes. It implements comparing transcripts while analyzing the alternative splicing, based on the modeling of exon gene graphs and efficient data mining algorithms. Use RNAexploreR to predict highly possible alternative transcripts of human genes.
