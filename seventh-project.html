Supervisor: Skakun V.V., Grinev V.V.

DEVELOPMENT OF A MODEL AND SOFTWARE FOR ANALYSIS OF ALTERNATIVE SPLICING OF PRIMARY MRNA OF HUMAN CHIMERIC ONCOGENE RUNX1 / RUNX1T1

Grant GPNI for 2016-2018 “Development of a model and software for analysis of alternative splicing of primary mRNA of human chimeric oncogene RUNX1 / RUNX1T1”, sub-program 12.3 “Combining” of program 12 “Convergence 2020”.

A model of the structural organization of the oncogene RUNX1 / RUNX1T1 in the form of an acyclic weighted digraph has been developed. Relevant signs were selected and a description of the whole variety of exons of the oncogene RUNX1 / RUNX1T1 was carried out. A new computational approach has been developed to predict alternative splicing events in the primary RNA of human genes. The idea of ​​the approach is to reduce the dimension of the exon trait space and combine closely located exons into a limited number of classes, replace the exon pathways of RNA generation with sequences of the corresponding exon class labels, calculate the distances between RNA transcripts, combine the closely spaced RNA objects into similar clusters. Using examples of model genes, the efficiency of the developed approach was confirmed and it was shown that the accuracy of RNA product separation is 99.5% for the considered pairs of model non-homologous human genes. An algorithm has been developed for generating alternative transcripts of the RUNX1 / RUNX1T1 oncogene as unweighted and weighted exon graph paths. It is shown that experimentally confirmed, as well as predicted transcripts do not form separate clearly separated clusters for human oncogene RUNX1 / RUNX1T1.

It has been established experimentally that the poor separability of experimentally confirmed and predicted RNA molecules of the RUNX1 / RUNX1T1 oncogene in their classification is due to the peculiarities of the generation of such transcripts in leukemic cells. It is shown that the main way of generating alternative RNA molecules of the oncogene under study is not the combinatorics of canonical exon variants, but the generation of a large number of alternative variants of such exons, and the ability to generate alternative isoforms in different exons is not the same. Finally, the RNAexploreR software package and a web application of the same name were developed, integrating all the implemented algorithms for the analysis of alternative splicing of human gene RNA products.

The solution of the problems presented by the R & D allowed for the first time to develop a set of algorithms and computer programs for constructing graph models of human genes and manipulating them when studying the patterns of alternative splicing. In addition, for the first time, the patterns of combinatorics of exons of the human RUNX1 / RUNX1T1 oncogene were established and some splicing rules were derived that lead to the formation of productive mRNA molecules. Finally, for the first time, the universality of the rules of productive combinatorics of exons was tested when splicing primary transcripts on a set of normal and mutant human genes. The results, due to their originality and novelty, are not only of fundamental fundamental importance, but can also find practical application in oncohematology in achieving the goals of a more accurate molecular biological diagnosis and management of human cancers.

According to the results, 8 articles were published in peer-reviewed scientific journals, 17 articles and abstracts at scientific conferences of various levels, and 5 Acts on the use of research results in the educational process and some guidelines for laboratory work were drawn up.
