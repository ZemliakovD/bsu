DEVELOPMENT OF MODELS, ALGORITHMS AND SOFTWARE FOR STUDYING TRANSCRIPTION REGULATION PROCESSES IN BACTERIA

When deciphering genomic sequences, complex computational tasks are associated with two stages of genomic projects: the assembly of a complete sequence from sequenced fragments and its annotation.

Annotation of genomic sequences involves the identification of coding sequences (open reading frames) with the determination of their possible functions, as well as the identification of regulatory sequences that control the expression of coding sequences: promoters, terminators, and transcription factor binding sites.

The identification of the most variable regulatory sequences, the binding sites of transcription factors (operators), is the most difficult. The task can be divided into two parts: (1) finding the regulatory motif (which actually comes down to building a mathematical model of the binding site for a specific transcription factor) and (2) scanning genomic sequences to find the coordinates of the operators corresponding to the motif (model).

The identification of regulatory sequences is of particular relevance for fundamental studies of intra- and intercellular regulation, as well as both fundamental and applied research related to the design of organisms with desired properties. The design of such organisms, whether it is a knockout line designed to study some physiological process or a bacterial producer strain, greatly benefits from modeling the regulation of the target trait. The presence of a correct regulation scheme makes it possible to directly obtain the desired quantitative changes by targeted manipulations with the levels of expression of regulatory genes and (or) with regulatory sequences recognized by the products of these genes.

The aim of the work is to develop models, algorithms, and software modules for the analysis of bacterial transcription factor binding sites (operators) and to create a web portal that integrates the analysis process. To achieve this goal, it is necessary to solve the following tasks:

Development of a database of operator and promoter motifs and a web interface to it. Development and verification of an algorithm for automatic calibration of threshold values ​​of models of regulatory motives;Creation of calibrated models of regulatory motives and filling the database; Development of an algorithm for identifying transcription units in bacterial genomes based on the analysis of promoters, operators, terminators and transcriptome data; Creation of an information-analytical web-portal for the analysis of regulatory sequences in bacterial genomes.
